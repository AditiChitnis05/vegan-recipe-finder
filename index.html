What is Vegan?

Veganism is a lifestyle and diet choice that excludes all animal products. This means avoiding meat, dairy, eggs, and other ingredients derived from animals. People choose veganism for many reasons‚Äîhealth, ethics, sustainability, and love for animals.

A Brief History of Veganism

The term "vegan" was coined in 1944 by Donald Watson and a group of non-dairy vegetarians in the UK who formed the Vegan Society. However, plant-based diets have existed for centuries in cultures around the world‚Äîfrom ancient Indian philosophies of ahimsa (non-violence), to Buddhist and Jain traditions that emphasize compassion toward all living beings.

Today, veganism has become a global movement driven by awareness of animal welfare, climate change, and the benefits of whole-food, plant-based nutrition.
